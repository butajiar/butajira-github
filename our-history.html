Our history

Memorable city history
moments

1926

  • 1926

    Butajira foundededddddddddd

    :

    The town of Butajira was founded. Missionary Pere Azaiz initially found nothing there.

  • 1935

    German expedition discovers town layout

    A German ethnographic expedition discovered a town laid out in straight lines and square shapes to serve as the administrative center of the Gurage people.

1937

  • February 24, 1937

    Execution of Ras Desta Damtew

    Ras Desta Damtew, a prominent figure, was taken prisoner in the small village of Eya. He was brought to Butajira and executed after a perfunctory trial on the same evening.

  • April 21, 1941

    Ethiopian flag raised over Butajira

    British patrols, as part of the East African Campaign during World War II, found that local resistance groups had dispersed Italian positions in the area. Both the British and Ethiopian flags were raised over Butajira on this day.

1994

  • 1994 Census

    Population recorded as 20,509

    According to the 1994 national census, Butajira had a total population of 20,509, with a majority practicing Islam

  • 1996

    Butajira designated as a reform city

    Butajira was designated as a reform city, signifying its strategic importance and potential for development. This designation likely led to increased investment and initiatives aimed at stimulating growth and progress.

  • 2007 Census

    Population increased to 33,406

    By the time of the 2007 census, Butajira's population had grown significantly, reaching a total of 33,406 individuals. This growth reflects the town's evolving demographic landscape and may indicate ongoing development and urbanization.
